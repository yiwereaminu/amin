FloodSafe Ghana - Early Warning System for Floods

The FloodSafe Ghana project is a groundbreaking initiative aimed at harnessing technology to safeguard lives and property from the devastating impact of floods in Accra, Ghana.

With a vision to create a resilient and prepared community, this project introduces an innovative early warning system designed to provide timely alerts, critical information, and resources during flood events.

Challenges Addressed: Frequent floods have plagued Accra, causing displacement of over 70,000 people in 2015 alone, and leading to significant damage to infrastructure and property. The FloodSafe Ghana project seeks to minimize these adversities by leveraging cutting-edge technology and strategic solutions.

Detection and Management of Rheumatic Disorders using Machine Learning and Blockchain.

Collaborating with fellow visionary Mohammed A. J. Kassim and under the expert guidance of Supervisor Godfrey A. Mills, this pioneering endeavor tackles the critical challenge of early identification and effective treatment of autoimmune rheumatic disorders.

Leveraging a powerful machine-learning-based decision support system, we achieved an impressive 97.48% accuracy in detecting common disorders such as Rheumatoid arthritis, Osteoarthritis, and Systemic Lupus Erythematosus. Our innovative approach extends beyond diagnosis, incorporating blockchain technology to secure patient data within a user-friendly web application

This trailblazing project stands as a testament to the seamless fusion of cutting-edge technology and medical innovation, poised to transform the landscape of healthcare diagnostics and patient care
